Tax & Compliance

It depends on your profit level and personal circumstances. Generally, if your profit exceeds £50,000, a limited company becomes more tax-efficient. However, there are other factors like liability protection, perceived credibility, and admin burden to consider. Use our tax calculator or book a consultation for personalized advice.

You must register for VAT if your turnover exceeds £90,000 in a 12-month period. You can also voluntarily register below this threshold if it benefits your business. We help you understand when to register and whether the Flat Rate Scheme could save you money.

You can claim any expense that is "wholly and exclusively" for business purposes. For digital businesses, this typically includes:

  • Software subscriptions and tools
  • Equipment (cameras, computers, microphones)
  • Home office costs (portion of rent, utilities, internet)
  • Professional fees (accountants, lawyers)
  • Marketing and advertising
  • Business travel and accommodation
  • Training and education related to your business

Making Tax Digital is HMRC's initiative to digitize the tax system. It's already mandatory for VAT-registered businesses and will soon apply to income tax self assessment. It requires using compatible software to keep digital records and submit returns. We use MTD-compatible software for all our clients.

For Content Creators

AdSense income is taxable as self-employment income in the UK. Google pays from Ireland, so there's no UK tax withheld. You must declare all AdSense earnings on your self assessment tax return. We help you track platform income, handle currency conversions, and optimize your tax position.

No, all creator income is treated the same for tax purposes - whether it's AdSense, sponsorships, brand deals, or donations. However, how you structure brand deals (invoice as individual vs company) can affect your tax efficiency. We help you optimize your income structure.

Yes! Equipment used for creating content is a legitimate business expense. This includes cameras, computers, microphones, lighting, editing software, and even things like ring lights or green screens. If equipment costs over £1,000, it's treated as a capital asset and claimed through capital allowances (often 100% in the first year).

For E-commerce Businesses

Inventory (stock) isn't deductible when you buy it - only when you sell it. We track your opening stock, purchases, and closing stock to calculate your true cost of goods sold. This gives you accurate profit margins and ensures correct tax calculations. We can integrate directly with your Shopify, Amazon, or other platforms.

Multi-channel selling requires careful reconciliation of sales, fees, and returns across platforms. We integrate with Shopify, Amazon, eBay, Etsy, and more to automatically consolidate your sales data. This ensures accurate reporting and helps you understand profitability by channel.

Post-Brexit, selling to EU customers has different rules. For goods over €150, you may need to register for VAT in the destination country or use OSS (One Stop Shop). For goods under €150 sent directly to consumers, you may need IOSS registration. We help navigate these complex rules and handle registrations.

Quick Tax Tips for Digital Businesses

1

Track Expenses Diligently

Keep receipts for all business expenses. Software subscriptions, equipment, home office costs, and travel are all deductible.

2

Consider Incorporation Early

Once your profit exceeds £50,000, a limited company often becomes more tax-efficient than sole trader status.

3

Save for Tax Payments

Set aside 30-40% of your income for tax. Use a separate savings account to avoid nasty surprises at tax time.

4

Understand VAT Thresholds

You must register for VAT if your turnover exceeds £90,000. Plan ahead to avoid penalties for late registration.
